System Architecture

Five-Stage Lifecycle of Recycled Aggregates

The White Carbon Circulation spans five interconnected stages. The conservation law of carbonate elements provides the theoretical foundation — calcium-based carbonatable elements in cement are conserved throughout all stages, enabling precise CO₂ quantification.

STAGE I · SERVICE STAGE
🏢 Concrete in Service Life
Concrete structures (buildings, bridges, infrastructure) absorb CO₂ naturally through surface carbonation during their service life. Fick's law governs carbonation depth.
CO₂ uptake peaks in 2058 at 0.3 GtC/year globally
STAGE II · PRODUCTION STAGE
🔨 Demolition → Recycled Aggregates
Waste concrete is demolished and crushed into Recycled Aggregates (RAs). Field data from 46 RA plants in China establish the carbon footprint benchmark.
Mean carbon emission: 2.47 kg CO₂/t  ·  60–90% from crushing
STAGE III · STORAGE STAGE
📦 Stockpiling with Natural Carbonation
RAs stockpiled at plant sites continue to react with atmospheric CO₂. Three packing modes (Pile / Tile / Accelerated) control sequestration efficiency.
Tile packing: 31× higher max sequestration than pile mode
STAGE IV · ACCELERATED CARBONATION
⚡ Industrial CO₂ Injection
High-concentration CO₂ environment with optimised temperature, humidity, and pressure dramatically accelerates carbonation, achieving theoretical maximum sequestration.
61.95 kg CO₂/t cement in 30 minutes (vs 0.01 kg pile mode)
STAGE V · RECYCLING STAGE
♻️ RAs → Recycled Aggregate Concrete
Carbonated RAs — with improved microstructure, filled cracks and pores via CaCO₃ crystallisation — are used to produce Recycled Aggregate Concrete (RAC), closing the loop.
CaCO₃ fills ITZ pores → improved durability + permanent carbon storage
Theoretical Foundation

Conservation Law of Carbonate Elements

The key theoretical innovation: calcium-based carbonatable elements in cement remain conserved throughout all lifecycle stages. By tracing original cement content, the maximum CO₂ sequestration potential can be precisely calculated — regardless of stage or region.

Core Carbonation Reactions

Calcium hydroxide carbonation
Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O
C-S-H carbonation (1)
(3CaO·2SiO₂·3H₂O) + 3CO₂ → (3CaCO₃·2SiO₂·3H₂O)
C-S-H carbonation (2)
(3CaO·2SiO₂) + 3CO₂ + nH₂O → 3CaCO₃ + SiO₂·nH₂O
Belite carbonation
(2CaO·SiO₂) + 2CO₂ + nH₂O → 2CaCO₃ + SiO₂·nH₂O
Maximum CO₂ sequestration per unit cement
46.24% – 55.81%
(theoretical maximum for fully hydrated cement-based materials)
⚗️
Element Conservation Principle
Calcium and magnesium ions — the primary carbonatable elements — originate from cement and remain conserved throughout all stages. The total CO₂ sequestration potential is bounded by original cement content, not environmental conditions.
🔬
Hydration Product Reactivity
In practice, maximum calcium ion reaction with CO₂ in fully hydrated cement is ~75%, as calcium ion dissolution becomes increasingly difficult when the Ca/Si ratio approaches 1. This sets realistic sequestration limits.
🌍
Global Applicability via USGS Data
Using global cement production data (USGS), the framework divides the world into four regions — China, USA, Europe, and Rest of World — enabling regional sequestration projections from 2020 to 2060.
📊
Monte Carlo Uncertainty Analysis
10,000-iteration Monte Carlo simulations with 13 key variables provide confidence intervals (50%, 68%, 80%, 90%, 95%) for annual CO₂ sequestration predictions across all modes and regions.
Storage & Carbonation Strategies

Three Carbonation Modes

From conventional pile storage to industrial accelerated carbonation, each mode offers dramatically different sequestration efficiencies. Optimising the packing strategy and carbonation conditions is the key lever to maximise the White Carbon Circulation.

⛰️
Mode I
Pile Packing
12.40 GtC
Cumulative 2020–2060
RAs stored in conical piles (standard practice). CO₂ penetrates only the surface layer due to restricted airflow. Carbonation limited to ~3× maximum aggregate particle size depth. Skewed uncertainty distribution — most sensitive to input variations.
▬▬
Mode II
Tile Packing
26.37 GtC
Cumulative 2020–2060
RAs spread flat in thin layers, maximising CO₂ contact area. Achieves theoretical maximum sequestration in 15 years (vs 25 for pile mode). Practical constraint: significantly larger land footprint per unit mass. Normal uncertainty distribution.
★ OPTIMAL
⚡
Mode III
Accelerated Carbonation
26.80 GtC
Cumulative 2020–2060
Industrial kiln with high-concentration CO₂, optimised temperature, humidity, and pressure. Complete carbonation within 3 months (assumed). Far outperforms natural modes — all particle sizes reach 77.32% carbonation degree.
61.95 kg CO₂/t cement in 30 minutes
vs 0.01 kg (pile) · vs 12.42 kg (tile)

Research Framework

Four Research Pillars

The White Carbon Circulation framework integrates four interconnected technical pillars, spanning materials science, structural engineering, environmental science, and artificial intelligence — forming a closed-loop full-chain technical system.

01
Deconstruction & Separation
低碳拆解与精细分离
Transition from extensive mechanical crushing to low-disturbance precision deconstruction. Static-pressure crushing, electric-pulse fragmentation, and PDE-driven multi-physics models clarify controllable fracture mechanisms for clean material separation.
Electric-Pulse Fragmentation Static-Pressure Crushing PINNs / PDE Models Digital Twins
02
Raw Material Upgrading & Reaction Pathway Reconstruction
原材料升级与反应路径重构
Multi-scale reactivity enhancement of RA/RP through CO₂ mineralization, nano-reinforcement, and bionic pore repair. AI-driven design of carbonation kinetics — from empirical matching to mechanism-driven low-carbon collaborative design.
CO₂ Mineralization CCC (Calcium Carbonate Concrete) Multi-scale Activity AI Material Design
03
Synergistic Performance Enhancement
协同性能提升机制
Dual objective of carbon sequestration and mechanical performance improvement. Interface strengthening, carbonation curing kinetics, fiber reinforcement, and multi-scale simulation enable interpretable predictions of concrete performance within the White Carbon system.
ITZ Strengthening Carbonation Curing Fiber Reinforcement ML / PDE Constitutive Models
04
Component Circular Design & White Carbon Evaluation
构件循环设计与白碳评价
Assembly–disassembly–reassembly circular model with FRP-reinforced demountable connections. Dynamic lifecycle LCA models, multi-parameter CO₂ absorption kinetics, and verifiable white carbon indicators (maintainability + traceability) for carbon-neutral certification.
Demountable Connections FRP Reinforcement Dynamic LCA White Carbon Accounting
